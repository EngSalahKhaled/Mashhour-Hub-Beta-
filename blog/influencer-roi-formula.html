"The campaign was successful, the influencer got great engagement!" This phrase is said daily in marketing meetings across the Gulf � while nobody actually knows if the campaign generated any real commercial return.

Why Is Influencer ROI Hard to Measure?

Influencer marketing mixes brand awareness and direct conversion � a complex blend that is difficult to separate and measure accurately without the right infrastructure built from the start.

The Practical Formula: Step by Step

  • Step 1 � Define the Measurable Goal: Direct sales (measured by unique coupon)? Site visits (measured by UTM)? New subscribers (measured by unique link)? Each goal has a different measurement method.
  • Step 2 � Calculate Total Cost: Influencer fees + cost of product provided + administrative time + cost of additional content.
  • Step 3 � Measure Attributed Revenue: Coupon sales + value of converted visits + value of new subscribers (based on average customer LTV).
  • Step 4 � Calculate ROAS: Revenue � Cost = return rate. Any number above 1 means technical profit, but factor in your product margin.

A Hidden Indicator: Cumulative Value

A successful influencer campaign is not measured only by immediate sales. The content the influencer produces stays on their platforms for years, generating awareness, being reshared, and influencing future purchase decisions that are never recorded in this month's report.
